## How to Use This Document

- Personalize `[INVESTOR NAME]`, `[FUND NAME]`, `[SPECIFIC PORTFOLIO CO]`, and `[MUTUAL CONNECTION]` fields before sending.
- Do not send all three emails in a sequence to the same person if you get a "no" reply — stop the sequence.

**Notes:**
- **YC-specific**: YC program managers review thousands of applications. This email should be factual, not persuasive. Its only job is to ensure your application doesn't get lost and that you seem like a sane, professional founder. Do not pitch — just provide context.

- Keep this under 200 words. Longer signals poor judgment about what program managers want.

[YOUR NAME]
[EMAIL]

---

**Notes:**
- **YC**: YC does not typically offer individualized feedback, but sending this email is low-cost and occasionally results in a one-line email back that contains a genuinely useful insight. Keep expectations low.
- **Techstars**: Techstars MDs are more likely to respond with brief feedback, especially for applicants who interviewed. If you made it to interview before rejection, mention it: "I appreciated the interview opportunity in [MONTH]."
- Do not argue with the decision. Do not explain why they got it wrong. One founder who sent a "here's why you're wrong to reject me" email to a YC partner described it as the single best way to never get in.

# Sending Checklist

Before sending any email in this sequence:

- [ ] Personalized `[INVESTOR NAME]`, `[FUND NAME]`, and any portfolio company references
- [ ] Verified the investor is still active (check their Twitter/X, recent portfolio announcements)
- [ ] Confirmed they invest at seed / pre-seed stage (not just growth)
- [ ] Removed any fabricated traction metrics — only include numbers you can defend
- [ ] Sent from a personal email address, not a marketing platform
- [ ] Set a calendar reminder for follow-up date (5 days for A-2, 10 days for A-3)
- [ ] Logged outreach in your CRM or tracking spreadsheet
